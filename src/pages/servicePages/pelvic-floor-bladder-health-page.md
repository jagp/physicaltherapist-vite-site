# Take Back Bladder Control

Leaking when you laugh, cough, or lift. Sudden urgency. Mentally mapping the nearest restroom everywhere you go. It's common — and it's treatable, often without surgery or medication.

## Stop planning your life around the bathroom

Pelvic floor physical therapy for bladder control works because the pelvic floor is at the center of it all. These muscles support bladder and bowel control, stabilize your core, and contribute to sexual function. When they're weak, tight, or simply uncoordinated, daily life gets disrupted — and that disruption is exactly what pelvic floor therapy is built to reverse, restoring both function and confidence.

This is care for all genders, across the lifespan. Whatever brought you here, the assessment is private and external-first — an internal evaluation is never mandatory, and everything moves at your pace.

## Treating the cause, not just the symptom

Bladder symptoms aren't all the same, and treating them well means telling them apart. **Stress incontinence** (leaking with pressure — a laugh, a cough, a lift) is different from **urge incontinence** and **overactive bladder** (sudden, hard-to-defer urgency), and both differ from urinary retention or **pelvic organ prolapse**. Each has its own drivers, and each responds to care aimed at the cause: pelvic floor retraining, behavioral strategy, and neuromuscular reeducation.

That approach matters because it so often works without surgery or medication. My core specialty is exactly this — as a Board-Certified Pelvic Health and Wellness Specialist (PWCS) and a former Brigham Urogynecology Coordinator, bladder and bowel disorders across the lifespan are the center of my practice, for patients of every gender. And you don't need a referral to start.

## Specialized treatments

- **Urinary Incontinence** — Stress, urge, and mixed leakage — conservative methods that often avoid surgery or medication.
- **Overactive Bladder (OAB)** — Urgency and frequency retrained via behavioral strategy and neuromuscular reeducation.
- **Urinary Retention** — Addressing muscle or neurological causes of incomplete emptying.
- **Pelvic Organ Prolapse (POP)** — Heaviness and pressure often improved without surgery.
- **Interstitial Cystitis / Painful Bladder** — Multimodal manual therapy, behavioral strategy, and education.

## Common questions

**Can physical therapy fix urinary incontinence without surgery?**
Very often, yes. Conservative pelvic floor therapy — retraining, behavioral strategy, and neuromuscular reeducation — treats the underlying cause of leakage and frequently resolves or greatly improves it without surgery or medication.

**Do men get pelvic floor therapy for bladder problems?**
Absolutely. The pelvic floor supports bladder control in every body, and this practice treats all genders. Men experience incontinence, urgency, and retention too — and the same cause-focused approach applies.

**How many sessions until bladder leakage improves?**
It varies with the cause and how long symptoms have been present, but many people notice meaningful improvement over a course of care as the pelvic floor is retrained. Your plan is individual, and we track progress together.
