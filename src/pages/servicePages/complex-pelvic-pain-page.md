# Break Free From Pelvic Pain

**"Complex" is not the same as "permanent."** Chronic pelvic pain has usually been going on for a while — often dismissed, worked around, or quietly endured. It doesn't have to be. There is almost always a source, and once we find it, there is almost always a way to calm it.

## You don't have to keep waiting it out

Pelvic floor therapy for chronic pelvic pain begins with something simple: taking the time to understand what is actually driving your pain. Chronic pelvic pain rarely comes from one place. It can involve muscle tension, nerve irritation, scar tissue, or some combination of the three — which is exactly why it so often goes unresolved when it's treated as a single problem.

It's also why intimacy quietly gets avoided. When something hurts, the body learns to protect itself, and sexual health becomes one more thing that feels off-limits. That's a physical pattern, and physical patterns can change. Here, everything is discussed openly, at your pace, with full consent — an internal exam is never mandatory, and nothing happens before you understand it and agree to it.

## Naming the pain is the first step to treating it

Persistent pelvic pain deserves a clinician who can name these syndromes without flinching and who has treated them for decades. As a Board-Certified Pelvic Health and Wellness Specialist (PWCS) with a background in complex pelvic floor dysfunction at Brigham, I assess where your pain is actually coming from — separating the muscular, nerve-driven, and scar-related contributors — and build a plan around that.

From there, treatment is targeted rather than generic: desensitization to quiet an over-sensitized system, hands-on manual therapy to release restricted tissue, and neuromuscular reeducation to help the pelvic floor work the way it's meant to. Conditions like **pudendal neuralgia**, **dyspareunia**, **vaginismus**, and **vulvodynia** respond to this kind of specific, methodical care — the goal throughout is restoring comfort and, where it matters to you, intimacy.

- **Vaginismus** — Highly treatable with progressive desensitization and pelvic floor retraining.

## Common questions

**Can pelvic floor therapy help painful sex (dyspareunia)?**
Yes. Painful intercourse usually has physical drivers — muscle guarding, tissue sensitivity, or scarring — that pelvic floor therapy can directly address. We work through them gradually, always at your pace, so intimacy can become comfortable again.

**What does treatment for pudendal neuralgia involve?**
Treatment focuses on calming an irritated nerve and the muscles around it. That means gentle desensitization, manual therapy to release surrounding tension, and neuromuscular reeducation — reducing the burning, shooting, or numb sensations over time rather than masking them.

**Is chronic pelvic pain treatable without medication?**
Often, yes. Because chronic pelvic pain frequently comes from muscle, nerve, and scar-tissue patterns, a targeted physical therapy approach can reduce or resolve it by treating those drivers directly — a conservative, first-line option worth trying before more invasive routes.
