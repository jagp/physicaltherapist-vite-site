# Reclaim Your Body After Cancer

Cancer treatment affects far more than the tumor. Survivorship isn't the end of your care — it's a stage of care in its own right, and it's when you get to rebuild strength, manage swelling, and feel at home in your body again.

## Recovery is part of the plan, too

Lymphedema therapy and cancer rehabilitation exist because treatment leaves its mark on more than one system. Surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy can affect your range of motion, your lymphatic system, and pelvic and sexual function — often in ways no one fully prepared you for. As a **Certified Lymphedema Therapist (CLT)**, I help survivors work through those effects and reclaim their bodies.

You may be dealing with a stiff, restricted shoulder after breast surgery. Or swelling that comes and goes. Or bladder and intimacy changes that caught you off guard. These are common, they're addressable, and they don't have to be the price you simply live with.

## Care shaped by exactly this work

The differentiator here is specific: I created and led Brigham's breast-oncology physical therapy team. That's a rare, focused background in precisely the recovery you're navigating — and it's a Partners in Excellence–recognized one.

For **breast cancer recovery**, that means post-surgical rehabilitation to restore range of motion, careful **scar tissue** management, and lymphedema prevention. For lymphedema itself, it means full certified-therapist care: **manual lymphatic drainage**, **compression**-garment fitting, and exercise prescription that actually fits your body. And because pelvic, bladder, and sexual side effects of surgery, radiation, or chemotherapy are real and rarely discussed, those are part of the conversation here too. Throughout, the pace is yours — survivorship-paced, gentle, and coordinated with your oncology team.

## Common questions

**What does a Certified Lymphedema Therapist do?**
A CLT is specially trained to prevent and manage lymphedema — the swelling that can follow cancer treatment. That care includes manual lymphatic drainage to move fluid, proper compression-garment fitting, and tailored exercise, all built around your stage of recovery.

**When should I start rehab after breast cancer surgery?**
Often sooner than people expect — early, gentle rehabilitation helps restore shoulder range of motion, supports healthy scar formation, and reduces lymphedema risk. We start where your body is and progress at a pace that respects your healing.

**Can physical therapy help side effects of radiation or chemo?**
Yes. Beyond lymphedema, treatment can bring tissue tightness, fatigue, and pelvic, bladder, or sexual changes. Physical therapy addresses these directly, helping you regain function and comfort as part of survivorship care.
