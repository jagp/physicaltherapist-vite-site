# Client Stylistic Suggestions

- v1.0

**Purpose:** A running list of copy and tone directives gathered directly from the client during copy review. Each entry is a declarative rule with a one-line rationale and, where useful, a before/after. Treat these as binding stylistic constraints for any site copy, alongside `design-philosophy.md` (north star: _calm authority_).

---

## Directives

### 1. No "plain-language" / "simplified" framing

**Do not** introduce content — especially links to outside clinical sources — with phrasing like _"For a plain-language look at…"_, _"in plain language…"_, or _"for a broader/simpler overview of…"_.

**Why:** It reads as condescending — it implies the reader needs the material dumbed down. The client flagged this specifically during review.

**But keep the authority signal.** The value of these outside-source callouts is that they align the practice with top-tier clinical institutions (Cleveland Clinic, Johns Hopkins, etc.) — that reflects the practitioner's own authority. Preserve that by keeping the clinical topic and the named institution; only change the lead-in.

**Before → After:**

- ❌ "For a plain-language look at how these muscles affect bladder and bowel control, Cleveland Clinic's overview is a good place to start."
- ✅ "For more on how these muscles affect bladder and bowel control, Cleveland Clinic's overview is a good place to start."

The named authority does the signaling; the intro should stay neutral.
