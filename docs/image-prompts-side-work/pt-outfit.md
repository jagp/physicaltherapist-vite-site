A more professional version would be a soft, tailored clinical outfit: slim straight-leg stretch trousers in charcoal, navy, deep olive, warm gray, or taupe; a smooth knit shell, fine-gauge sweater, or simple mock-neck top in cream, sage, slate blue, muted plum, or ivory; and a short structured jacket, washable blazer-cardigan, or collarless work jacket with clean seams and discreet pockets. The layer should look intentional and polished but still allow the practitioner to sit, kneel, demonstrate exercises, and move around a treatment table without restriction. Shoes would be clean leather or knit clinical sneakers in white, cream, gray, or soft tan, chosen for support rather than sportiness. Accessories should be restrained: small earrings, a simple watch, short nails, no dangling necklace, no jangly bracelets, and hair neatly pulled back, clipped, or shaped away from the face.
